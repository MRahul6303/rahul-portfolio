## Overview

I led the end-to-end design and structural architecture of a comprehensive Project Operations Management System built to unify every department involved in delivering construction and signage projects. The company previously relied on spreadsheets, informal communication, and untracked approvals. My goal was to create a single platform that guides a project from creation to closeout with clarity, traceability, and real-time visibility.

## The Problem

Projects involved multiple teams—Engineering, Design, Production, QS, Finance, QC, and Logistics—each operating independently. Information was scattered, version control was inconsistent, and approvals were difficult to track. This caused delays, miscommunication, and an inability to monitor overall progress.

## The Solution

I designed a modular, workflow-driven system that standardizes every stage of project execution. The platform centralizes data, automates approvals, manages documents, and visually tracks status across all departments.

### Key Features & Modules

**Project Creation Flow**
A guided multi-step intake (Project Info → Department Assignment → Review & Submit) with permission settings, document uploads, financial details, and clean validation.

![Project creation flow with multi-step intake process](/images/projects/onboarding-conversion-uplift/project-creation-flow.png)

**BOQ & Costing**
Modern tables with image references, Excel import, mismatch detection, and release modals for factory allocations—ensuring quantity accuracy and financial transparency.

**Engineering & Mockups**
Task assignment with revisions, comments, rework cycles, QC feedback, and mockup review drawers supporting version history and client digital signatures.

![Project Management Dashboard overview showing unified system architecture](/images/projects/onboarding-conversion-uplift/dashboard-overview.png)

**Production Management**
Modern tables with image references, Excel import, mismatch detection, and release modals for factory allocations—ensuring quantity accuracy and financial transparency.

**Engineering & Mockups**
Task assignment with revisions, comments, rework cycles, QC feedback, and mockup review drawers supporting version history and client digital signatures.

**Production Management**
Overview, Detailed, and Released views with expanded statuses (Not Started → Installed), inline release actions, factory indicators, tooltip hints, and lifecycle dates.

**Reworks Module**
A standalone modal showing issue images, QC comments, before/after comparisons, and threaded communication—allowing engineers and QC to collaborate clearly.

**RFIs, Payment Applications & IPCs**
Redesigned financial and communication tables with status chips, document links, and cumulative calculations for payment transparency.

**Deliverables & Closeout**
Submission and closeout sections that track client-facing documents, required approvals, file references, and digital signatures, ensuring smooth project handover.

## Impact

The system replaced fragmented manual processes with a unified digital workflow. Teams now work from one source of truth, approvals are traceable, documentation is consistent, and leadership gains real-time visibility. Errors reduced dramatically, accountability increased, and project cycles became noticeably faster.

## My Role

I owned the entire UX architecture, interaction design, workflow mapping, UI system creation, status logic, and inter-department process definition. I produced detailed specifications, designed all components (tables, modals, drawers), and ensured every feature fit into a coherent, scalable system.
